Tiger Jungle Art Camp Hosts 50+ Artists Across Four Stages

Mycelium Music & Art Festival returns to Tiger Jungle Art Camp in Chiang Dao for its 2025 edition, transforming a jungle clearing 70 kilometers north of Chiang Mai into a three-day celebration of electronic music, world fusion, and Thailand's thriving underground scene.[1][2] The festival takes its name from mycelium‚Äîthe underground fungal network connecting plants in symbiotic communication‚Äîa fitting metaphor for the interconnected community of Thai and international artists gathering at the foot of Chiang Dao's mountains.

This isn't another beach party festival. Mycelium creates a temporary village where music and art feel stitched into the soil itself, where Bangkok's electronic music pioneers share stages with Berlin techno producers, where Karen folk traditions meet modular synthesis, and where Thailand's premier reggae force performs under open sky while psytrance spirals through the graveyard shift until sunrise.[3] Over two nights and three days, four distinct stages unfold, each with its own temperament and carefully curated sound palette.

Srirajah Rockers

SAT 23:00‚Äì00:00 | Mycelium Main Stage

If you see only one act at Mycelium 2025, make it Srirajah Rockers. This 12-piece reggae and dub powerhouse represents the absolute pinnacle of Thailand's reggae scene, backed by credentials that dwarf nearly every other act on the lineup. With over 20 years of continuous evolution spanning five generations of members and 83,522 Facebook followers, Srirajah Rockers have earned their position as Thailand's most popular band among young people.[9][12]

The numbers tell part of the story: five studio albums including "ENDURO," released just this January 2025, demonstrate sustained creative output over two decades.[11] But it's their festival r√©sum√© that cements their must-see status. They've performed at Wonderfruit Festival alongside international heavyweights Rudimental and Nightmares on Wax, at Maho Rasop Festival sharing stages with Tame Impala and The Drums, and regularly command crowds at Big Mountain Music Festival in Khao Yai.[10]

What makes Srirajah Rockers distinctive isn't just technical proficiency or reggae authenticity‚Äîit's their fusion approach, blending reggae and dub foundations with traditional Thai sounds to create something both true to its Jamaican roots and distinctly Southeast Asian.[9] When twelve musicians lock into their groove under the Mycelium Stage's open sky at midnight on Saturday, you're witnessing the culmination of twenty years of sonic experimentation that has defined a generation of Thai music.

The festival circuit validation backs up the critical recognition. Elaheh

SAT 02:00‚Äì04:00 | Jungle Stage

When the Jungle Stage hits 02:00 Saturday morning, the crowd that remains will witness one of Bangkok's most respected selectors in her element. Elaheh commands a two-hour slot that represents both recognition of her status and the perfect timeframe for her deep, cosmic, psychedelic approach to house and techno.[16]

She's an integral part of UNST, described as "a local institution that focuses on the deeper shades of house and techno," while simultaneously serving as a resident of Soundistan, characterized as "a forward-thinking and leftfield-driven movement."[16] She co-founded UNST alongside DJs DOTT and Sarayu, and also co-created Collect/Save, a project dedicated to helping up-and-coming DJs learn their craft.[18]

The 02:00 to 04:00 Saturday slot represents the sweet spot for her sound. The crowd has self-selected by this hour‚Äîcasual festival-goers have retreated, leaving dancers committed to the journey. This is without a doubt one of the city's truly forward-thinking selectors performing in optimal conditions.[16]

Helicopter Secondhand

FRI 19:30‚Äì20:30 | Mycelium Main Stage

Helicopter Secondhand, a trio from Chiang Mai, carries the distinction of representing Thailand at the Busking World Cup 2023‚Äîan achievement that validates their live performance prowess on an international stage.[19] The three-piece consists of Pod on lead vocals and guitar, Bas on drums and percussion, and Jack on bass and didgeridoo.

The band blends contemporary Western pop-rock with rich musical flavors from Africa, the Americas, and traditional sounds from Thailand's rural areas, creating what observers describe as an "intriguing fusion."[19] They've performed at Bangkok Design Week and the Kunming Coffee Festival in China, demonstrating appeal beyond their Chiang Mai home base.

Wannarit (Stylish Nonsense)

FRI 01:00‚Äì02:00 | Grounding Stage

Wannarit Pongprayoon brings 20 years of electronic music pioneering to a one-hour set that promises the kind of improvisation-heavy approach that built his reputation.[22][23] Best known as one half of Stylish Nonsense (formed in 1993), his impact extends through Panda Records, the label he founded and has operated for over 16 years.[24]

Multiple sources describe him as a "pioneer of Thai electronic music" whose work through both Stylish Nonsense and Panda Records has shaped what experimental electronic music sounds like in Thailand.[22][23] The Grounding Stage at 01:00 Friday represents the perfect context for his improvisational electronic explorations.

Casper

SAT 04:00‚Äì06:00 | Jungle Stage

When the Jungle Stage reaches 04:00 Saturday morning, the festival delivers one of its most significant bookings: Casper, the Chiang Mai-based maestro whose two-hour sunrise set represents both a homecoming and a masterclass.[26] In 2004, he initiated "Puretek," which evolved into WU (Wualai Underground), among the first movements to introduce underground electronic music to Chiang Mai.

His trademark "groove" travels fluidly between progressive house, tech-house, and techno.[26][28] The 04:00 to 06:00 slot carries profound significance‚Äîwhen sunrise begins transforming the jungle from shadow to light, Casper's deep, groovy techno serves this transition perfectly.

Klee Bho

SAT 20:00‚Äì21:00 | Mycelium Main Stage

Klee Bho's one-hour set represents something deeper than entertainment‚Äîit's a mission to preserve and celebrate Karen (Pkakenyaw) cultural heritage through music that focuses on the spirit of nature, solitude, community ways of life, family, and love.[20] The artist delivered a TED Talk titled "Of Culture, Roots and Music," signaling that this performance carries educational and cultural weight beyond typical festival sets.[21]

Klee Bho identifies as Pwa Ka Nyaw, a Karen ethnic group that actively fights to conserve forests, and explicitly frames his music as presenting "songs of the forest, nature and traditions."[21] Saturday at 20:00 represents prime festival time when people are receptive and engaged.

Four Stages Create Distinct Sonic Territories

Understanding Mycelium 2025 requires understanding its four-stage architecture, where careful curation ensures each stage maintains sonic coherence while offering genuine alternatives to whatever else is happening simultaneously.[3]

Mycelium Main Stage

Full-band performances: rock, folk, reggae, and world music. Where Srirajah Rockers commands midnight, Venn delivers experimental alt-folk, and Helicopter Secondhand brings their Busking World Cup credentials. 12 live band slots across the weekend.

Jungle Stage

Continuous DJ sets from Friday afternoon through Sunday evening. 16 total slots, each running two hours. Where Elaheh spins deep house at 02:00 Saturday, Casper commands the sunrise, and Bangkok's underground scene represents.

Psysquare Stage

The graveyard shift psytrance haven. Six sets from Saturday evening through Sunday afternoon covering goa, forest, dark, and twilight sub-genres. For psytrance devotees who want deep immersion without interruption.

Grounding Stage

Ambient and experimental refuge. 11 sets designed for recovery, contemplation, and sonic exploration. Where Wannarit brings experimental textures, Pyrosoma performs with modular systems, and sound healing meets progressive ambient.

Essential Festival Intelligence

Location & Access

Tiger Jungle Art Camp sits in Chiang Dao, approximately 70 kilometers north of Chiang Mai City‚Äîabout an hour's drive under normal conditions.[5] Chiang Dao functions as "a hidden gem, far removed from the crowds of Chiang Mai, home to cool local artist festivals, gorgeous glamping in nature and some of the freshest produce in Thailand."[6]

Camping & Accommodation

The festival explicitly encourages camping: "Set up your camp in festival, meeting with friends from around the world, exchange cultural experiences, and immerse yourself in unique encounters you won't find anywhere else."[8] November in Northern Thailand brings cool nighttime temperatures, particularly in the mountains. Pack layers and a quality sleeping bag rated for 15¬∞C/60¬∞F minimum.

What to Bring

  • Insect repellent (DEET-based, 30%+ concentration) ‚Äì non-negotiable in jungle environments
  • Headlamp or flashlight with spare batteries for nighttime navigation
  • Layers for temperature changes ‚Äì warm days, cool nights (15-25¬∞C range)
  • Reusable water bottle for hydration and environmental responsibility
  • Cash ‚Äì withdraw in Chiang Mai before departure, limited ATM access onsite
  • Comfortable walking shoes ‚Äì you'll cover significant distance on jungle terrain
  • Sun protection ‚Äì hat, high-SPF sunscreen, sunglasses
  • Supplemental snacks ‚Äì energy bars, nuts, dried fruit

Food & Services

"Various shops provide services, including food and beverages, offering both vegetarian and general cuisine to cater to your needs throughout the event."[8] Expect vendor pricing to run higher than outside options, with selection potentially narrowing late at night.

Beyond the Music

The festival includes a "Natural Art Exhibition" showcasing "diverse artworks created through a connection with nature by various talented artists."[8] Nearby attractions include Pha Plong temple ("a peaceful temple in Chiang Dao valley situated on the hillside, can be reached by walking up 509 stairs"[5]) and Chiang Dao Cave (historical site from the mid-1400s).
